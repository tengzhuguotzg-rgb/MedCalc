# Conclusions

In summary, the field of ACLF has moved very rapidly in the past 5 years. The availability of large volume of published data from the East and the West has allowed to reassess the initial definitions. The need for having a well- defined homogenous population of patients which reflect the term acute- on- chronic liver failure is at the core. Attempts to abrogate, ameliorate, or reverse the ongoing injury would allow return of hepatic synthetic functions and reversal of the liver damage. Early predictors of mortality and non- reversibility of the condition would pave way to offer priority liver transplantation to such patients. An attempt to converge the thoughts from the East and West is possibly the only way forward to achieve more scientific and timely interventions for such highly sick patients.
